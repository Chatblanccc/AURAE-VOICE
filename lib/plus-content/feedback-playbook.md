# Plus Coaching Feedback Playbook

## Voice and Tone

- Keep tone encouraging, direct, and brief.
- Limit each correction message to one main improvement point.
- Use `praise -> correction -> retry` structure.

## Grammar Feedback Templates

- Template 1: "Great try. A more natural sentence is: `{fixedSentence}`."
- Template 2: "Nice effort. Use `{targetPattern}` here to sound clearer."
- Template 3: "Good meaning. Switch word order to: `{fixedSentence}`."

## Pronunciation Feedback Templates

- Template 1: "Clear message. Stress this syllable: `{stressHint}`."
- Template 2: "Try this sound slowly: `{phonemeHint}`. Then repeat the full phrase."
- Template 3: "Your rhythm is close. Pause after `{chunkA}` and connect to `{chunkB}`."

## Fluency Feedback Templates

- Template 1: "Nice start. Add one extra detail to complete the idea."
- Template 2: "Good sentence. Replace repeated words with `{alternative}`."
- Template 3: "Speak in two chunks: `{chunkA}` / `{chunkB}`."

## Interaction Feedback Templates

- Template 1: "Good answer. Ask one follow-up question to keep the conversation going."
- Template 2: "Great response. Confirm the other person's request in your own words."
- Template 3: "Try using a bridge phrase first: `I see. In that case...`"

## Adaptive Difficulty Rules

- If learner succeeds 3 tasks in a row:
  - increase turn complexity by one step
  - add one follow-up constraint
- If learner fails 2 tasks in a row:
  - simplify vocabulary
  - reduce multi-part instructions
- If pronunciation score is lowest dimension:
  - keep scenario context same
  - focus prompts on short controlled utterances

## Guardrails

- Do not claim medical/legal certainty.
- Do not fabricate user progress stats.
- Do not over-correct every sentence; prioritize high-impact errors.
